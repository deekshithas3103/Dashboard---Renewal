Bright Spot

tedBook is the only tier that renewed

2 of 5 tedBook customers (40%) renewed — comfortably above the 22% overall rate. Both renewals came from tedBook, and both happened during the back-to-school window (August) or mid-cycle review point (February).

tedBook: 40% · Overall: 22% · Lift: 18 percentage points
Concern

StepUp and SLH both went 0%

StepUp: 0 of 2 (both no response). SLH: 0 of 2 (one decline, one no response). Combined, the Online and SLH families produced zero Indiana renewals last year — a complete miss outside of tedBook.

StepUp + SLH combined: 0 of 4 · 0% renewal rate
Headline

Renewals clustered in August and February

Both renewals fell in months where customer engagement is naturally high — back-to-school (August) and mid-year review (February). The "No Response" months (Sep, Mar, May) overlap with assessment season and quieter periods.

Aug & Feb: 100% of renewals · Sep, Mar, May: 80% of no-responses
Caveat

Small sample, but the pattern aligns

9 customers is a thin sample, but the pattern aligns with broader program findings: tedBook drives engagement and renewal, StepUp lags in Indiana specifically, and silence (not rejection) is the dominant churn mechanism.

Direction of travel matters more than precise rates at this scale.

The Looming Risk for SY 2026–27

Risk Alert · SY 2026–27 Renewal at Risk

If nothing changes, the 26–27 renewal cohort enters the cycle with zero engagement evidence.

Renewal isn't decided at renewal time — it's decided by what happens between purchase and renewal. Six new customers in 25–26, all unrostered. None trained. None using the platform. The 26–27 renewal cycle will be deciding whether to renew customers who never actually used what they bought. That conversation rarely goes well.

Critical Gap

No rostering means no usage data at renewal time

When 26–27 renewal conversations open, we'll have nothing concrete to point to. No login activity. No assessments completed. No teacher engagement data. The customer has no felt experience of the platform — only the workbook on the shelf.

Renewal without engagement evidence = renewal at full friction.
Compounding Issue

Spring acquisitions are the hardest to activate

4 of the 6 new customers arrived in March, April, or May — past the natural rostering windows and well into assessment season. Even with full effort, these customers have very little of the school year left to log meaningful usage.

Mar–May acquisitions: 4 customers · Time to act before EOY: minimal
What History Tells Us

Silence in 24–25 predicts silence in 26–27

The 56% "No Response" rate last year wasn't random — it was the natural outcome of customers who never built a relationship with the platform. We're set up to repeat it unless we intervene before SY 25–26 closes.

Pattern: unrostered → unengaged → unresponsive at renewal time.
The Opening

Seven months is still time to act

The 26–27 renewal window opens around July 2026. That's seven months from now to roster these 6 customers, run training sessions, prove usage, and create the engagement story that 24–25 customers never had. Doable — but only if action starts now.

Target: 100% rostered by Jan 2026 · Trained by Mar 2026 · Usage data by May 2026

SY 2026–27 Strategic Plan

Six moves to reverse the 24–25 pattern and arrive at the 26–27 renewal window with engagement evidence in hand. Built directly from the data and from broader program findings.

1

Default to tedBook+360

Indiana confirms what national data already proved: tedBook is the only renewing tier in this state. Make tedBook+360 the standard offering for every new Indiana sale. Retire +Starter and +Coach as default SKUs at the next pricing cycle.

tedBook: 40% renewal · Other tiers: 0%
2

Roster all six new customers by January

Every one of the 6 new 25–26 customers must be rostered by end of January 2026. Direct CSM outreach, white-glove onboarding, and weekly check-ins until rostering is complete. Without rostering, there is no path to 26–27 renewal.

Current: 0 of 6 rostered · Target: 6 of 6 by Jan 31, 2026
3

Deploy One-Click from January

For any 25–26 customer not rostered by end-January, run the One-Click Practice Test recovery sequence proven in Experiment №01. It woke up 22 of 102 dormant schools nationally. Even partial uptake here would change the 26–27 conversation.

National precedent: 22% dormant-school activation
4

Solve the No Response problem

56% of 24–25 renewals went silent. Build a structured renewal cadence: 90-day, 60-day, 30-day touchpoints with multi-channel outreach (email + call + LMS notification). Track response rate as a leading indicator — silence is the enemy, not refusal.

Target: cut "No Response" below 30% in 26–27
5

Investigate StepUp & SLH gaps

Both StepUp and SLH went 0 of 2 in Indiana last year. Before renewal conversations open, talk to those four customers — not for renewal, but to understand the failure. Their answers will shape how we sell, support, or sunset those tiers in Indiana.

4 customer interviews · Target completion: Dec 2025
6

Concentrate outreach in Aug & Feb

Both 24–25 renewals fell in August and February. These are the windows where Indiana school leaders are reachable and renewal-minded. Plan the 26–27 outreach calendar around them — front-load conversations into Aug 2026 and Feb 2027.

Aug + Feb = 100% of historical Indiana renewals
The 26–27 Goal
Lift Indiana renewal rate from 22% (baseline) to 50%+ in SY 2026–27 — by entering renewal season with rostering, training, and usage evidence for every customer in the cohort.

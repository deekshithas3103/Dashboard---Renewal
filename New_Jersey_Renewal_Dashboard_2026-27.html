Star Performer

StepUp is renewing at 50% — the highest in the program

12 of 24 StepUp customers in NJ renewed last year. That's not just the best StepUp performance we've seen — it's the best single-tier performance in any state we've analyzed. Only 2 customers actively declined; the other 10 non-renewals were all "no response," meaning there's more upside if we can reach them.

StepUp NJ: 50% renewal · Only 2 explicit declines out of 24
Solid Backbone

tedBook is the volume anchor at 40%

52 of 86 customers (60% of the NJ cohort) are on tedBook, and 21 of those renewed — a 40% rate. This is materially better than tedBook performance in every other state we've analyzed. Whatever's happening in NJ sales and CS motion for tedBook, it's working.

tedBook NJ: 40.4% renewal · 3x higher than tedBook rate in Oklahoma
Weak Spot

SLH lags all other tiers

10 SLH customers, only 2 renewed. 4 explicitly declined and 4 went silent — 80% total loss. This is out of pattern with the rest of the NJ portfolio and warrants investigation. Whatever's working for tedBook and StepUp here isn't translating to SLH.

SLH NJ: 20% renewal · Only tier underperforming state average
Pattern

Renewals cluster Aug–Feb, especially February

Peak months: Feb (8 renewals), Nov (6), Aug (4). These are the natural conversation windows in NJ — back-to-school, mid-year planning, and legislative-cycle month. The Feb spike is particularly striking: 8 of 16 customers due that month renewed. Concentrate 26–27 outreach here.

Feb + Nov + Aug: 18 of 35 renewals (51%)

The Looming Risk for SY 2026–27

Opportunity Alert · The Case for Aggressive Targets

NJ is our strongest state — and we're leaving 20+ renewals on the table each year.

Unlike Indiana or Oklahoma, NJ doesn't have a broken funnel — it has a great funnel with specific gaps. Last year, 30 customers went silent. This year, 5 SLH customers are unrostered. And 5 rostered customers are flagged as at-risk. Each of these is knowable, addressable, and worth real money. The risk isn't the state falling apart; the risk is complacency stopping us from scaling what already works.

The Playbook

StepUp NJ is the template — study it

50% renewal in 24–25, 94% activation in 25–26, 45% renewal rate on this year's cohort so far. Something specific is working with StepUp in New Jersey — sales positioning, CS motion, product-market fit, or all three. Document it. Codify it. Export it to Oklahoma and Ohio where StepUp also shows promise.

StepUp NJ: 94% rostered · 91% trained · 85% using · 45% renewing
The Blind Spot

All 5 new SLH customers are unrostered

SLH already underperformed at 20% renewal last year. This year, none of the 5 new SLH customers have been rostered. If we don't intervene, we're on track for a repeat of 24–25 SLH results — and possibly worse. Small cohort, but easy to address.

SLH 25–26: 5 acquired · 0 rostered · 2 of 10 renewals achieved
Watch List

5 customers flagged at-risk

Of the 61 rostered customers, 5 have been marked at-risk (3 StepUp, 1 tedBook, 1 pending). These are customers we can still save — they're on the platform but showing early signals of trouble. Immediate CSM intervention on these five should be a priority before Q1 2026.

5 at-risk = potential 5 additional renewals if we intervene now
The Compounding Effect

NJ is a laboratory for scaling

With 90 new customers this year, NJ has more scale to test operational improvements than any other state. Anything we discover here about closing the no-response gap, converting at-risk customers, or accelerating SLH activation can be applied to smaller markets with immediate impact.

NJ = 90 new customers · IN + OH + OK combined = 41 · NJ is 2.2× the rest

SY 2026–27 Strategic Plan

Six moves built around New Jersey's strength: a proven playbook to scale, specific gaps to close, and the market volume to make every improvement meaningful.

1

Document and codify the StepUp NJ playbook

50% renewal, 94% activation — StepUp NJ is the most successful state-tier combination in the program. Interview the CSMs, sales reps, and 2–3 top customers driving this. Document the specific motions that work. This becomes the reference playbook we export to other markets.

Target: playbook complete by Nov 2025
2

Close the "no response" gap — the biggest lever

30 silent customers in 24–25. Even converting 30% of them would add 9 renewals to the SY count. Structured multi-channel outreach cadence (90/60/30-day), with response rate tracked as a leading indicator. If we could hit 55% renewal here, NJ becomes a benchmark case.

Target: cut "No Response" from 35% to 20% in 26–27
3

Roster the 5 SLH customers by January

Small cohort, big signal. All 5 new SLH customers are unrostered — the only complete blind spot in the NJ pipeline. Direct CSM intervention on this cohort: personalized outreach, white-glove onboarding, weekly check-ins. Prevents 26–27 from repeating the 24–25 SLH pattern.

Current: 0 of 5 SLH rostered · Target: 5 of 5 by Jan 2026
4

Intervene on the 5 at-risk customers now

5 customers are already flagged at-risk despite being rostered. These are the most reversible losses — early warning signs mean there's still time to save them. Assign a senior CSM to each of the 5, run a 30-day recovery play, and document what works for future use.

5 at-risk customers · Recovery target: 3 of 5 saved by Feb 2026
5

Concentrate outreach on Feb, Nov, Aug

NJ renewals concentrate in three months: Feb (8 renewals), Nov (6), Aug (4). Build the 26–27 outreach calendar around these windows. Front-load high-touch conversations 30 days before each, ensure CSM capacity is high in these periods, and treat them as sales-critical months.

Feb + Nov + Aug: 51% of historical NJ renewals
6

Set an aggressive but reachable target

Most states are climbing from 15–22% renewal to 40%+. NJ is already at 40% and should be aiming for 55%+. With the no-response fix (+9 renewals), at-risk recovery (+3), and SLH activation (+2), the math actually gets us there. Make this the flagship state for 26–27.

Baseline 41% · Target 55% · Gap: 14 renewals across 90+ customers
The 26–27 Goal
Lift New Jersey renewal rate from 41% (baseline) to 55%+ in SY 2026–27 — by scaling the StepUp playbook, closing the silence gap, and treating NJ as the reference market other states will learn from.

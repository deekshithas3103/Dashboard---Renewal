Bright Spot

StepUp is the strongest tier in Oklahoma

3 of 9 StepUp customers renewed — a 33% rate, more than double the state average. This is the inverse of what we see nationally and in other states, where tedBook tends to lead. Something about the Oklahoma market favors the Online product, and that signal is worth pursuing.

Major Concern

tedBook is broken in Oklahoma

13 tedBook customers, only 1 renewed — a 7.7% rate. 9 of 13 (69%) explicitly declined. This is the worst tedBook performance in the dataset by a significant margin. Whatever's wrong, it's not a national product issue — it's an Oklahoma-specific mismatch.

tedBook OK: 7.7% renewal · 69% explicit non-renewal · Worst in dataset
Headline

The combination of high tedBook decline rate plus high overall decline rate suggests a structural issue — possibly tied to Oklahoma state assessment requirements, district purchasing cycles, or competitive products dominant in the state.

The Looming Risk for SY 2026–27

Risk Alert · The Oklahoma Paradox

Strong activation, weak retention — we're winning the wrong half of the funnel.

Oklahoma's operational execution in 25–26 is impressive: 86% rostered, 79% trained, 86% using the platform. But last year, 50% of customers still explicitly declined to renew. This combination is unusual and concerning — it suggests the problem isn't engagement or activation. It's something else entirely: product fit, pricing, district policy, or competitive displacement. Strong activation in 25–26 buys us a much better conversation, but only if we identify and address the underlying decline drivers.

SY 2026–27 Strategic Plan

Six moves built around Oklahoma's specific reality: an active-rejection-dominant churn pattern, StepUp outperforming tedBook by 4×, and a strongly-activated 25–26 cohort that's the envy of every other state.

1

Make StepUp the default for new OK sales

Oklahoma is the inverse of the national pattern — StepUp renews at 33% here while tedBook trails at 7.7%. For new sales in this state, lead with StepUp instead of tedBook. This is a state-specific positioning decision that goes against our broader strategy, and that's exactly what the data demands.

OK StepUp: 33% renewal · OK tedBook: 7.7% renewal
2

Investigate why tedBook fails in Oklahoma

9 of 13 tedBook customers explicitly declined last year — the worst tedBook result in any state. Before renewal conversations open, conduct structured interviews with the 13 tedBook customers (renewed and not). The answers will determine whether to fix, reposition, or sunset tedBook in Oklahoma.

Target: 10+ tedBook interviews by Nov 2025
3

Protect the 25–26 activation gains

86% rostered, 79% trained, 86% using — Oklahoma is operationally the strongest state in the dataset. Don't break this. Continue weekly check-ins with the 12 active customers, keep training pipeline running through May, and document usage outcomes for the renewal conversation.

Current: 12 of 14 active · Hold above 80% through SY end
4

Win-back campaign for the 13 decliners

Unlike silent customers, the 13 explicit decliners are knowable, reachable, and have shared why they're leaving (or we can ask). Build a structured win-back outreach for SY 26–27 — referencing their stated reasons and offering specific changes. Even a 15% recovery rate adds 2 renewals.

Target: 2 win-backs from 24–25 decline cohort
5

Build a Jan–Feb renewal calendar

3 of 4 Oklahoma renewals came in Jan–Feb, not the typical Aug back-to-school window. This matches state district planning cycles. Front-load 26–27 outreach into January and February. Schedule major customer touchpoints in Q3 SY to align with how Oklahoma districts actually buy.

Jan–Feb: 75% of historical OK renewals
6

Investigate state-level structural factors

The combination of high explicit decline, inverted tier preference, and Jan–Feb renewal pattern points to something specific about Oklahoma. Likely candidates: OSTP assessment alignment, state-mandated digital tools, or a strong local competitor. Research before designing 26–27 sales motion.

Output: Oklahoma-specific market brief by Dec 2025
The 26–27 Goal
Lift Oklahoma renewal rate from 15% (baseline) to 40%+ in SY 2026–27 — by pivoting to StepUp-led positioning, addressing tedBook's structural fit issues, and leveraging the strongest activation cohort in the entire program.
